01 / About

Notes from 17 years in the trees.

Tropical forest ecologist. Founder of the Canopy Stratification Lab at the Universidade Federal do Amazonas. Working at the seam between canopy ecology, ethnobotany, and conservation policy.

I have spent the last seventeen years thirty meters above the forest floor, suspended in canopies that almost no human has ever stood under. My work sits at the seam between tropical ecology, ethnobotany, and the politics of conservation — questions I first met as a child in the Putumayo, listening to my grandmother name the trees in two languages neither of us could fully translate.

Today I lead the Canopy Stratification Lab at the Universidade Federal do Amazonas, with field stations in Manaus, Iquitos, and the lower Sepik. The lab maps vertical biodiversity — the way a single hectare of rainforest stacks five forests on top of each other — and asks what we lose, exactly, when the tallest of them dies first.

When I am not in the trees I am writing about them, or photographing them, or arguing with ministries on behalf of them. I believe the canopy is the last room we will ever discover on this planet, and I am trying to describe it before the door closes.

02 / Research

Field work, in progress.

Five active programs across three continents. Each is a long answer to a small question about how a forest holds itself together. Scroll →

01 / 052019—present

Vertical Forests of the Rio Negro

A six-year vertical stratification census across 18 hectares of igapó floodplain, building the first complete inventory of bromeliad-borne fauna in the upper canopy. Funded by the Gordon and Betty Moore Foundation.

Location · Amazonas, BrazilStatus · ActiveStage · Field
02 / 052021—present

Ayahuasca Vines & the Limits of Translation

Co-led with elders of the Cofán Nation. A long-form ethnobotanical study of Banisteriopsis caapi cultivars — the names, songs, and pharmacologies that travel with them — and a working paper on benefit-sharing under the Nagoya Protocol.

Location · Putumayo, ColombiaStatus · ActiveStage · Analysis
03 / 052022—2025

The Mycorrhizal Atlas

A three-continent mapping of below-ground fungal networks linking emergent canopy trees, in collaboration with SPUN. The project quantifies, for the first time, mycorrhizal connectivity loss in selectively logged forests.

Location · Borneo / Sepik / ManúStatus · ActiveStage · Field
04 / 052017—2023

Last Light: A Census of Emergents

Identifying and tagging every canopy emergent above 45 meters across 12,000 hectares of primary forest. The dataset informed the 2024 IUCN reassessment of three Couratari species.

Location · Manú National Park, PerúStatus · ActiveStage · Analysis
05 / 052020—present

The Sound of a Healthy Canopy

A bioacoustic study using 240 autonomous recording units across four continents to define a baseline soundscape signature for intact tropical forest. Open-source datasets released annually.

Location · Pan-tropicalStatus · ActiveStage · Field
